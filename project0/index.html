Architectural Perspective Compression

I took two photos of a pretty tree down a sidewalk. In the first one, I am standing far away and zoom into the subject. In the second, I walk up close with an unzoomed lens in order to try and match it to the first image. Zooming in increases focal length, which flattens perspective and compressed distance. The up close photo gives more depth and perspective points.

The Dolly Zoom

To roughly emulate the dolly zoom effect in film, I have taken four successive photos of a set of plants on a table. After each photo, I took a few steps back while zooming in with the camera in order to preserve the image size. I then compiled them into a GIF, seen below.
